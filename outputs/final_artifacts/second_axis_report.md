### The Developmental Story

Rather than a straight line, the lifecycle of an attention head looks like a branching tree:

1. **The Trunk (Sink $\to$ Local):** As heads mature, their $V/Q$ ratio steadily increases, and their entropy collapse neutralizes (approaching $\Delta \approx 0$). They transition from structural boundaries (Sink) to general local gathering (Local).
2. **The Branching Point:** Once a head becomes a mature Local head, it hits a developmental crossroads. To specialize further, it must bifurcate along the functional entropy axis.
3. **Branch A (Retrieval):** Some heads push their $V/Q$ ratio to the absolute maximum and develop a strong **positive** entropy collapse ($\Delta > 0.30$), firing sharply only when a specific semantic match is found in the deep past.
4. **Branch B (Induction):** Other heads pull their $V/Q$ ratio slightly back and develop a strong **negative** entropy collapse ($\Delta < -0.50$), firing sharply to execute precise character-level sequence copying.

### Scientific Implications

This disproves the hypothesis of a single, uniform developmental pipeline across all functional dimensions. Instead, it proves that **Local heads are the universal stem cells of the transformer.** 

The network develops generalized Local heads early on, and then structurally diverges them into highly specialized, diametrically opposed functional roles (Retrieval vs. Induction) in the late stages of the sequence.
