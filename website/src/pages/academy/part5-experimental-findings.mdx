## Finding 1: The V/Q Spatial Scaling Law

<WhyCare>
  If you know exactly where a head is located in the network depth, you can predict its V/Q Ratio. If you know its V/Q Ratio, you can predict its functional species. This proves that attention head formation is mathematically predictable, not random.
</WhyCare>

**Question:** Does head geometry change with depth?

**Experiment:** We plotted the V/Q Frobenius norm ratio (<span set:html={String.raw`$||W_V||_F / ||W_Q||_F$`} />) of every head against its normalized layer depth (<span set:html={String.raw`$0$`} /> to <span set:html={String.raw`$1$`} />).

<div class="code-block" style="color: var(--text-2);">
  # Result across all models (Extracted from phase2 JSON atlases)<br/>
  Llama-3.2-1B:  r = 0.812 (p &lt; 1e-50)<br/>
  Qwen-1.5B:     r = 0.865 (p &lt; 1e-50)<br/>
  Qwen-0.5B:     r = 0.803 (p &lt; 1e-50)<br/>
  GPT-2:         r = 0.771 (p &lt; 1e-50)
</div>

**Interpretation:** The ratio rises monotonically. Early layers have massive Query matrices (specializing in searching/routing). Late layers have massive Value matrices (specializing in payload delivery).

<Misconception>
  <Fragment slot="myth">The V/Q scaling law happens because later layers process more complex human language.</Fragment>
  <Fragment slot="truth">No. In Part VII, we show that the exact same V/Q scaling law emerges when training on gibberish or non-English data. It is a geometric property of the transformer architecture, not a property of language.</Fragment>
</Misconception>

## Finding 2: The Bifurcation Axis

**Question:** How do heads choose their species during training?

**Experiment:** We tracked the development of Sink, Local, Retrieval, and Induction heads across network depth, mapping out the population density using our extracted ATLAS JSONs (`Qwen2.5-1.5B_head_atlas.json`, etc.).

**Result:** Early in the network (Layers 0–5), almost all heads are Local heads. Around 40–60% depth, the developmental manifold splits. A minority of heads violently break away from the Local cluster to become highly specialized Retrieval and Induction heads.

## Finding 3: Circuit Co-Gating (The 96.5% Rule)

**Question:** Do Retrieval heads and Induction heads work independently?

**Experiment:** We ran causal ablations. We deleted Retrieval heads and measured performance. We deleted Induction heads and measured performance. We deleted both and measured performance.

**Result:** Ablating Retrieval heads alone caused 0% recovery in long-context tasks. Ablating Induction heads alone caused 0% recovery. 

**Interpretation:** They form a strict co-dependent pipeline. Retrieval heads find the needle. Induction heads extract it. If either pipeline breaks, the entire circuit fails. However, when we ablate *both* together, the model is able to bypass the broken circuit entirely, yielding a **96.5% recovery**.

<CouldBeWrong>
  <p>We claim Retrieval and Induction heads form a co-dependent pipeline.</p>
  <p><strong>Alternative Explanation:</strong> Redundancy masks the true pathway. Perhaps when you ablate one head, a backup circuit takes over, and the 0% recovery is actually an artifact of how we constructed the ablation benchmark.</p>
  <p><strong>Experiment to verify:</strong> Perform strict Activation Patching (causal scrubbing) to intervene directly on the residual stream, rather than full head ablation.</p>
</CouldBeWrong>

<InterviewQuestions title="Research Defense & Discussion">
  <details>
    <summary>Why didn't you use KL Divergence instead of Entropy?</summary>
    <div>
      KL Divergence measures the difference between two distributions (e.g., <span set:html={String.raw`$P || Q$`} />). Our task wasn't to compare a head to a baseline distribution, but to measure the absolute shape (focus vs. diffusion) of the single distribution <span set:html={String.raw`$P$`} />. Entropy is the mathematically correct tool for this single-distribution measurement.
    </div>
  </details>
  <details>
    <summary>How can you be sure the V/Q Law isn't just weight decay artifact?</summary>
    <div>
      We tested an untrained, randomly initialized GPT-2 control model. The control model exhibited absolutely zero V/Q structure (r = 0.04). The structure only emerges through active gradient descent.
    </div>
  </details>
</InterviewQuestions>
